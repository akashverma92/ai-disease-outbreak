Pertussis

Overview

Pertussis, commonly known as whooping cough, is a highly contagious respiratory disease caused by the bacterium Bordetella pertussis. It is characterized by severe coughing fits that can make it difficult to breathe. Vaccination is crucial, especially for infants and young children, to prevent serious complications. The DTaP vaccine is recommended for children, while the Tdap booster is recommended for adolescents and adults.

Key Numerical Data

  • In 2018, there were approximately 16,000 reported cases of pertussis in the U.S.
  • Pertussis can lead to severe complications, particularly in infants under one year of age.
  • Vaccination coverage for pertussis is around 90% in children.
