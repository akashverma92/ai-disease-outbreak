Small-Pox

Overview

Smallpox is a highly contagious and deadly disease caused by the variola virus, characterized by fever, body aches, and a distinctive rash. Smallpox was declared eradicated in 1980 due to an effective vaccination campaign led by the World Health Organization. The smallpox vaccine is no longer routinely given, as the disease is no longer a threat.

Key Numerical Data

  • Smallpox was the first disease to be eradicated through vaccination.
  • There were an estimated 300 million deaths due to smallpox in the 20th century before its eradication.
  • The last natural outbreak of smallpox occurred in 1977.
