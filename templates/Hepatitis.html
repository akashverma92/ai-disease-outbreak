Hepatitis

Overview

Hepatitis refers to an inflammatory condition of the liver, primarily caused by viral infections (Hepatitis A, B, C, D, and E). It can also be caused by non-infectious factors such as alcohol consumption, autoimmune diseases, and toxins. Symptoms can range from mild to severe and may include jaundice, fatigue, abdominal pain, and loss of appetite. Chronic hepatitis can lead to liver cirrhosis and liver cancer. Vaccination is available for Hepatitis A and B, while Hepatitis C has effective antiviral treatments.

Key Numerical Data

  • Estimated 325 million people live with chronic hepatitis B and C worldwide.
  • Hepatitis A has about 1.4 million new cases annually.
  • Vaccination coverage for Hepatitis B is around 85% in infants globally.
